How to Play

Win: capture the enemy capital, or research every tech.

Pinch your unit — selects it. Pinch any reachable tile and it walks the shortest path; enemies in range are red and pinching them attacks.

Pinch the same unit again — opens its menu (Found City, Build Mine/Farm, Fortify, Skip).

Pinch your city — opens the city screen (production, food, prod).

Pinch empty ground — when units have moves left it opens the global menu; when they don't it just ends the turn.

Esc — cycles cursor to the next unit with moves.

Scroll mode — toggle with ↑ ↓ ↑ ↓. Arrows then pan the map even into unexplored areas.

Zoom — cycle with ← → ← →: far · normal · close.

Factions: Solaris (+1 food per city), Umbra (+1 atk on military), Tellus (+1 gold per city). You and two rivals start on the same map.

Ages: Ancient → Classical (4 techs) → Medieval (7) → Modern (10). Advancing gives a gold bonus.

Later units: Swordsman (Steel), Catapult (Engineering, siege), Musketman (Gunpowder, strong ranged).

Later buildings: Aqueduct (+3 food), Temple (+3 sci), Bank (+4 gold).

Borders grow with city population. Working a tile requires your culture. Defending in your own borders gives +10%.

Tribal villages — three-tent encampments. First unit in claims a one-shot reward: gold, free worker, research, or a population bump.

Barbarian raiders — grey warriors that spawn from unowned edges in the early game. Weaker than your warriors. Walls + Fortify shut them down.

World Wonders — once-per-game buildings in the city menu marked with ✦. Race the AI: lose the race and you get half your invested production back.

Natural wonders — Volcano gives +1 prod to adjacent worked tiles; Geyser gives +1 food to adjacent.
